Mueller, a former professional hockey player,  was drawn to poker after one of many long road trips during his hockey career. After retiring in 1999 from hockey he has cashed in many poker tournaments throughout his poker career. 
Mueller went on to win his second World Series of Poker bracelet and $194,909 only 11 days after his first in a $1500 Limit Hold'em Shootout event.
His best finish in the World Poker Tour was a fourth place in the 2006 World Poker Challenge where he won $142,285. 
Mueller is a representative of Full Tilt Poker.
As of 2010, his total live tournament winnings exceed $1,750,000. His 19 cashes at the WSOP account for $1,516,206 of those winnings.
