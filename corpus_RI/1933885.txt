The '''Sylvania Sports and Exhibition Center''' is a convention center located in Sylvania, Ohio.  It contains  of exhibit space in the fieldhouse.  The fieldhouse contains the hardwood basketball floor used at the 1994 NBA All-Star Game at the Target Center in Minneapolis, Minnesota.
The Center shares the Tam O'Shanter complex with two ice rinks (capacities: 1100 and 700), and a senior center.  It is used for conventions, trade shows, and sports.
The Center also took over the Children's Wonderland in 2010 which used to be held at the Lucas County Recreation Center
