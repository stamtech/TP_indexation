Gay Mitchell was defeated by Enda Kenny in the Fine Gael leadership election, 2002.
His late brother Jim Mitchell was a long-serving Fine Gael TD and former government minister.
Gabriel "Gay" Mitchell was born in Inchicore, Dublin in 1951. He was educated at St. Michael's Congregation of Christian Brothers, Emmet Road Vocational School, Dublin Institute of Technology College of Commerce and Queen's University Belfast. 
He first became involved in politics in 1979 as a member of Dublin City Council. He was first elected to Dail Eireann as a Fine Gael TD in the 1981 general election for the Dublin South Central constituency.
Following Michael Noonan's resignation as Fine Gael leader in 2002, Gay Mitchell was a challenger in the subsequent leadership election, losing out to the victor, Enda Kenny. Before his election to the European Parliament, Gay Mitchell had been party spokesperson on Foreign Affairs, writing the party's "Beyond Neutrality" policy document.  
In November 2006, Gay Mitchell announced that he had taken the difficult decision not to contest the 2007 general election and concentrate on his European seat.
During the 2008 referendum on the Treaty of Lisbon, Gay Mitchell was Fine Gael's director of elections for the referendum. 
Gay Mitchell is a member of the European Parliament Committee on Development, the Special Committee on the Financial, Economic and Social Crisis and the delegation to the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly. He is also a substitute member of the Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs and the delegation for relations with the People's Republic of China.
Gay Mitchell is the author of "By Dail account", the first book published in Ireland on the history and role of the Public Accounts Committee (PAC) and the great office of Comptroller and Auditor General.
In 2011, he announced his intention to seek the Fine Gael nomination in the race for Ireland's presidency.
