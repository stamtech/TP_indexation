It was formed on April 1, 1869, from parts of Morgan, Lawrence, and Carter counties.
According to the 2000 census, the county has a total area of , of which  (or 99.47%) is land and  (or 0.53%) is water.
Elliott County has voted for the Democratic Party's nominee in every Presidential election since it incorporated in 1869. This is the longest ongoing streak of any county voting Democratic in the United States.
Elliott County was the second whitest county in the country, at 99.04%, to vote for Barack Obama in the 2008 Presidential election, the whitest being Mitchell County, Iowa. Obama garnered 61% of the vote, while John McCain received 36%. In 2008, Elliott county provided Obama with the highest percentage of the winning vote out of all Kentucky counties.
