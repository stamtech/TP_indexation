Fort Mitchell was the site of  American Civil War fortifications built for the Defense of Cincinnati in 1862 named for  General Ormsby M. Mitchel, a professor at Cincinnati College (now the University of Cincinnati).
Fort Mitchell was chartered as a city in 1909.
As of the census of 2010, there were 8,207 people, 3,530 households, and 2,033 families residing in the city. The population density was 2,581.8 people per square mile (997.8/km  ). There were 3,744 housing units at an average density of 1,195.0 per square mile (461.8/km  ). The racial makeup of the city was 96.87% White, 0.99% African American, 0.10% Native American, 0.80% Asian, 0.02% Pacific Islander, 0.47% from other races, and 0.74% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 0.85% of the population.
